The Power of Shared Experiences

Support groups bring together people facing similar challenges. By sharing experiences, strategies, and hope, members find strength in community and learn they are not alone in their struggles.

Regular attendance at support groups is associated with better recovery outcomes for many people dealing with gambling problems.

Types of Support Groups

Gamblers Anonymous (GA)

A 12-step fellowship of men and women who share their experience, strength and hope with each other to recover from compulsive gambling.

Format: In-person and online meetings
Cost: Free (voluntary contributions)
Approach: 12-step program
Find GA Meetings

GamAnon

Support groups specifically for family members, partners, and close friends of problem gamblers.

Format: In-person and online meetings
Cost: Free (voluntary contributions)
Approach: Mutual support and education
Find GamAnon Meetings

SMART Recovery

A science-based program helping people recover from addictive behaviors, including problem gambling.

Format: In-person and online meetings
Cost: Free
Approach: Self-empowerment and cognitive behavioral techniques

Virtual Support Options

Can't make it to in-person meetings? Many groups now offer online alternatives:

  • Zoom and Skype meetings
  • Phone support lines
  • WhatsApp and Telegram groups
  • Moderated forums and chat rooms

These virtual options provide the same level of confidentiality and support as in-person meetings.

Attending Your First Meeting

Feeling nervous about attending a support group for the first time is completely normal. Here's what to expect:

  • You'll be welcomed, but not pressured to share
  • You can simply listen if you prefer
  • Everything shared in meetings is confidential
  • You'll likely hear stories that resonate with your experiences
  • Most people report feeling a sense of relief after their first meeting

Remember: Everyone in the room has been where you are now. Taking that first step is the hardest part.
